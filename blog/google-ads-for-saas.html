Google Ads for SaaS: How to Run Profitable Campaigns

March 11, 2026 • 14 min read

Google Ads can be incredibly profitable for SaaS—or a complete money pit. The difference is strategy. Here's how to run campaigns that actually generate ROI.

Why Google Ads for SaaS?

  • Intent-based: People are actively searching for solutions
  • Scalable: Increase budget as you prove ROI
  • Measurable: Clear attribution from click to close

Campaign Structure

1. Branded Campaigns

Bid on your own brand name. Cheap clicks, protects against competitors, high conversion rate.

2. Competitor Campaigns

Bid on competitor brand names. Higher CPCs but captures people evaluating alternatives.

3. Non-Branded Campaigns

Generic keywords related to your solution. Higher volume, lower intent than branded.

Keyword Strategy

Focus on high-intent keywords:

  • Best for B2B: "[category] software", "[category] platform", "[category] tool"
  • Comparison: "[competitor] vs [competitor]", "[competitor] alternative"
  • Problem-specific: "how to [solve problem your product solves]"

Bidding Strategies

  1. Start with manual CPC: Control while learning
  2. Graduate to Target CPA: Once you have conversion data
  3. Test Target ROAS: For mature accounts with revenue tracking

Landing Page Best Practices

  • Match headline to search intent
  • Single clear CTA
  • Social proof above the fold
  • Fast load time (under 3 seconds)
  • Mobile-optimized

Common Mistakes

  1. Too many keywords: Start focused, expand what works
  2. Sending to homepage: Use dedicated landing pages
  3. Ignoring negative keywords: Block irrelevant searches
  4. Not tracking conversions: Set up proper conversion tracking
  5. Giving up too early: Need 100+ conversions for optimization

Budget Guidelines

For B2B SaaS:

  • Minimum test budget: $3,000-5,000/month for 2-3 months
  • Expected CPC: $5-20 for B2B keywords
  • Target CPL: Depends on ACV, but $50-200 typical
