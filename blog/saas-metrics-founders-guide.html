15 SaaS Metrics Every Founder Should Track in 2026

By Great Hornbills Team • March 2026 • 10 min read

Why SaaS Metrics Matter

SaaS businesses live and die by their metrics. Unlike traditional businesses, SaaS companies have unique economics: recurring revenue, customer lifetime value, and the compounding effects of retention. Understanding these metrics is essential for making smart decisions about growth, pricing, and investment.

Revenue Metrics (1-5)

1. MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)

Formula: Sum of all recurring revenue from active subscriptions
Why it matters: The heartbeat of your SaaS business. Track total MRR and break it down by new, expansion, contraction, and churned MRR.

2. ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)

Formula: MRR × 12
Why it matters: Used for annual planning and valuations. Most investors talk in ARR terms.

3. ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)

Formula: MRR / Number of Customers
Why it matters: Shows pricing power and customer segmentation. Rising ARPU indicates successful upselling.

4. Net Revenue Retention (NRR)

Formula: (Starting MRR + Expansion - Contraction - Churn) / Starting MRR
Why it matters: NRR above 100% means you grow even without new customers. Best SaaS companies hit 120%+.

5. Gross Revenue Retention (GRR)

Formula: (Starting MRR - Contraction - Churn) / Starting MRR
Why it matters: Shows pure retention without expansion. GRR can never exceed 100%.

Customer Metrics (6-10)

6. CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)

Formula: Total Sales & Marketing Spend / New Customers
Why it matters: The cost of acquiring each customer. Must be recovered through customer lifetime value. Read our guide to reducing CAC.

7. LTV (Lifetime Value)

Formula: ARPU × Gross Margin × Customer Lifespan
Why it matters: Total revenue expected from a customer. LTV:CAC ratio should be 3:1 or higher.

8. CAC Payback Period

Formula: CAC / (ARPU × Gross Margin)
Why it matters: Months to recover acquisition cost. Target under 12 months for healthy unit economics.

9. Monthly Churn Rate

Formula: Lost Customers / Starting Customers
Why it matters: Customer churn under 2% monthly is good for B2B. Revenue churn matters more than logo churn.

10. Activation Rate

Formula: Users Reaching "Aha Moment" / Total Signups
Why it matters: Predicts long-term retention. Define your activation event and optimize onboarding.

Growth Metrics (11-15)

11. MRR Growth Rate

Formula: (Current MRR - Previous MRR) / Previous MRR
Why it matters: Monthly growth rate. Early-stage SaaS should grow 15-20%+ monthly.

12. Quick Ratio

Formula: (New MRR + Expansion MRR) / (Churned MRR + Contraction MRR)
Why it matters: Measures growth efficiency. Quick ratio above 4 is excellent.

13. Conversion Rate (Trial to Paid)

Formula: Paying Customers / Free Trial Signups
Why it matters: For freemium/trial models, aim for 2-5% freemium or 15-25% free trial conversion.

14. Lead Velocity Rate (LVR)

Formula: (Current Month Leads - Previous Month Leads) / Previous Month Leads
Why it matters: Leading indicator of future revenue growth.

15. Rule of 40

Formula: Revenue Growth Rate + Profit Margin ≥ 40%
Why it matters: Balances growth and profitability. Benchmark for healthy SaaS businesses.

Building Your Metrics Dashboard

Don't track everything — focus on metrics that drive decisions. Start with:

  • Weekly: MRR, New signups, Activation rate
  • Monthly: CAC, LTV, Churn, NRR
  • Quarterly: LTV:CAC, Rule of 40, Cohort analysis

Use tools like ChartMogul, ProfitWell, or Baremetrics for automated tracking.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is MRR?
MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue) is the predictable revenue a SaaS business generates each month from subscriptions. Calculate it by summing all recurring revenue from active subscriptions, excluding one-time fees.
What is a good churn rate for SaaS?
For B2B SaaS, aim for monthly churn under 2% (annual churn under 10%). Enterprise SaaS often achieves under 1% monthly churn. For B2C/SMB SaaS, 3-5% monthly churn is common but still challenging for growth.
How do you calculate LTV?
LTV = ARPU × Gross Margin × Average Customer Lifespan. For example: $100 ARPU × 80% margin × 24 months = $1,920 LTV. You can also use: ARPU × Gross Margin / Monthly Churn Rate.
What is Net Revenue Retention (NRR)?
NRR measures revenue retained from existing customers including expansions and contractions. NRR over 100% means you're growing revenue from existing customers even without new sales. Best-in-class SaaS companies achieve 120%+ NRR.
What's the Rule of 40?
The Rule of 40 states that a SaaS company's growth rate plus profit margin should equal or exceed 40%. For example, 30% growth + 10% profit margin = 40. It balances growth vs. profitability.
