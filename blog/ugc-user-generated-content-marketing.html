UGC Marketing: How User-Generated Content Drives Brand Growth

By Great Hornbills Team • March 2026 • 6 min read

What Is User-Generated Content (UGC)?

User-Generated Content (UGC) is any content — photos, videos, reviews, testimonials, or social media posts — created by real customers or fans rather than the brand itself. UGC is the most trusted form of marketing content, with 79% of consumers saying UGC highly impacts their purchasing decisions, compared to just 13% for brand-created content.

Why UGC Is Essential for Modern Brands

UGC provides authentic social proof that builds trust with potential customers. It reduces content creation costs by leveraging your community as a content engine. UGC also performs exceptionally well in paid advertising campaigns — UGC-based ads generate 4x higher click-through rates and 50% lower cost per acquisition than traditional brand creative.

Types of UGC and How to Collect Each

Customer reviews and testimonials: Request after purchase via automated emails. Social media posts: Create branded hashtags and repost user content. Unboxing and product videos: Encourage with packaging inserts. Before-and-after photos: Ideal for beauty, fitness, and home improvement brands. Video testimonials: Offer incentives for detailed video reviews.

Building a UGC Strategy

Create a branded hashtag, implement a UGC collection workflow, secure usage rights before repurposing content, and integrate UGC across your marketing channels. Combine UGC with micro-influencer partnerships for maximum impact.

UGC Best Practices and Legal Considerations

Always get explicit permission before using customer content in your marketing. Credit the original creator when reposting. Have clear terms and conditions for any UGC campaign or contest. Never edit UGC in ways that misrepresent the original creator's intent.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does UGC stand for?

UGC stands for User-Generated Content. It refers to any content — including photos, videos, reviews, testimonials, and social media posts — created by real customers, fans, or users of a product or service rather than by the brand itself.

How do I get customers to create UGC?

Make it easy and rewarding. Create branded hashtags, include sharing prompts in packaging and post-purchase emails, run contests and giveaways, feature customer content on your channels (people love being featured), and consider offering small incentives like discounts or loyalty points.

Can I use customer photos and videos in my ads?

Only with explicit permission. Always request usage rights before repurposing any customer content in paid advertising, website content, or other marketing materials. Many brands include UGC usage rights in their contest terms and conditions or request permission via direct message.
