Product-Led Growth (PLG): The Complete Guide for SaaS

March 11, 2026 • 15 min read

Slack, Figma, Notion, Calendly—the most successful SaaS companies of the last decade share one thing: they're product-led. Here's everything you need to know about PLG.

What is Product-Led Growth?

Product-Led Growth is a go-to-market strategy where the product itself is the primary driver of customer acquisition, conversion, and expansion. Users experience value before they pay.

Sales-Led

  • Demo → Trial → Close
  • Sales controls the journey
  • High CAC, high ACV
  • Longer sales cycles

Product-Led

  • Try → Use → Upgrade
  • User controls the journey
  • Lower CAC, variable ACV
  • Faster time to value

The Three PLG Models

1. Freemium

Free forever tier with limited features. Users upgrade for more capabilities. Works when free tier delivers real value without cannibalizing paid.

Examples: Slack, Notion, Figma

2. Free Trial

Full product access for limited time. Creates urgency to convert. Works when value is clear within trial period.

Examples: Shopify, HubSpot, Salesforce

3. Reverse Trial

Start on paid plan, downgrade to free if they don't convert. Psychological advantage—users experience full value first.

Examples: Ahrefs, some enterprise tools

PLG Metrics That Matter

  • Time to Value (TTV): How long until users experience the "aha moment"?
  • Activation Rate: % of signups who complete key actions
  • Free-to-Paid Conversion: % of free users who become paid
  • Product-Qualified Leads (PQLs): Users whose behavior indicates buying intent
  • Net Revenue Retention: Are existing customers expanding?

Building for PLG

  1. Identify your "aha moment": What action shows a user gets value?
  2. Reduce friction to that moment: Remove every obstacle
  3. Build for self-serve: Users should succeed without talking to anyone
  4. Design natural upgrade points: Limits should feel natural, not punitive
  5. Instrument everything: You can't optimize what you don't measure

When PLG Doesn't Work

PLG isn't for everyone. Consider sales-led if:

  • Your product requires significant customization
  • Value takes weeks/months to realize
  • Buyers aren't the users
  • ACV is $50K+ (though PLG can still help)
  • Security/compliance requires procurement

PLG + Sales (The Hybrid Model)

The best companies combine PLG with sales. Let PLG handle acquisition and qualification, then bring in sales for expansion and enterprise deals.

Slack, Zoom, and Atlassian all use this hybrid approach—PLG for land, sales for expand.
