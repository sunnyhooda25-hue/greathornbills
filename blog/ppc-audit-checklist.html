PPC Audit Checklist: 25 Points to Optimize Your Ad Campaigns

By Great Hornbills Team • March 2026 • 6 min read

Why Regular PPC Audits Matter

Most PPC accounts waste 20-40% of their budget on underperforming keywords, audiences, and placements. A systematic audit identifies these leaks and uncovers optimization opportunities. Conduct a full audit quarterly and mini-audits monthly for both Google Ads and Meta Ads.

Account Structure Audit (Points 1-7)

1. Verify conversion tracking is firing correctly. 2. Check that Google Analytics 4 is properly linked. 3. Review campaign structure and naming conventions. 4. Audit ad group theme tightness. 5. Check for keyword overlap between campaigns. 6. Review geographic targeting settings. 7. Verify device bid adjustments are appropriate.

Keyword and Targeting Audit (Points 8-14)

8. Review search terms report for irrelevant queries. 9. Update negative keyword lists. 10. Check keyword match types. 11. Audit Quality Scores and identify improvement areas. 12. Review audience targeting and exclusions. 13. Check for audience overlap between ad sets. 14. Verify remarketing audiences are properly populated.

Ad Creative and Landing Page Audit (Points 15-20)

15. Review ad copy relevance and CTR by ad. 16. Check that ads are using all available extensions. 17. Verify ad rotation settings. 18. Audit landing page relevance and load speed. 19. Check landing page mobile experience. 20. Review A/B test results and pause underperformers.

Budget and Bidding Audit (Points 21-25)

21. Check for campaigns limited by budget. 22. Review bid strategy effectiveness and learning phase status. 23. Audit cost per conversion by campaign, ad group, and keyword. 24. Check ROAS against targets and budget allocation. 25. Review impression share and competitive metrics.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I audit my PPC campaigns?

Conduct a comprehensive audit quarterly and mini-audits monthly. Daily monitoring should cover budget pacing, conversion tracking, and any sudden performance changes. Weekly reviews should check search term reports, ad performance, and audience engagement. The depth of audit increases with frequency: daily spot-checks, weekly reviews, monthly mini-audits, quarterly deep dives.

What is the most common PPC mistake?

The most common mistake is not regularly reviewing search term reports and adding negative keywords. This leads to wasted spend on irrelevant searches. Other frequent mistakes include not testing ad creative, using broad match keywords without proper negative keywords, and not tracking conversions properly.

Can I audit my PPC accounts myself?

Yes, using the 25-point checklist above. However, an outside perspective often catches issues that internal teams become blind to. A professional audit typically identifies 20-40% budget waste that can be reallocated to higher-performing areas. Consider professional audits annually even if you manage day-to-day optimization internally.
