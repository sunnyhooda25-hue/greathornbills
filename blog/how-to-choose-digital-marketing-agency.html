How to Choose the Right Digital Marketing Agency: 10 Questions to Ask

By Great Hornbills Team • March 2026 • 6 min read

Why Choosing the Right Agency Matters

Your digital marketing agency becomes an extension of your team. The wrong agency wastes your budget and time, while the right agency transforms your marketing into a revenue engine.

Questions 1-3: Expertise and Experience

1. What specific results have you achieved for similar businesses? Request case studies with concrete metrics. 2. Which channels are you strongest in? An honest agency acknowledges specialties. 3. Who will work on my account, and what is their experience level?

Questions 4-6: Process and Communication

How does their onboarding work? A good agency sets realistic expectations — typically 3-6 months for SEO results and 1-3 months for paid advertising optimization. How frequently will you communicate? Do they use templates or custom strategies?

Questions 7-8: Pricing and Contracts

What is included in pricing — strategy, execution, creative, reporting? What are contract terms? Agencies confident in their work offer month-to-month agreements. Use our budget planning guide to determine your optimal investment.

Questions 9-10: Culture and Values

How do they stay current with changes? Can you speak with 2-3 current clients? Ask references about communication quality, results delivered, and how the agency handles underperformance.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much should I pay a digital marketing agency?

Agency pricing varies widely. Small agencies charge $1,000-$5,000/month, mid-size agencies $5,000-$15,000/month, and large agencies $15,000-$50,000+/month. Your budget should align with your revenue goals — most businesses invest 7-12% of revenue in marketing. At Great Hornbills, plans start at $999/month.

What should I expect from a marketing agency in the first 3 months?

Month 1: Onboarding, audit, and strategy development. Month 2: Campaign launch, initial optimization, and baseline metrics. Month 3: Performance analysis, strategy refinement, and early results. Most agencies show meaningful traction by month 3 for paid channels and month 6 for organic channels.

Should I hire a full-service agency or specialists?

Full-service agencies are better for most small-to-mid-size businesses because they provide coordinated strategy across channels. Specialist agencies may be better for large enterprises with specific, advanced needs in one channel. Consider your team's internal capabilities and which channels matter most for your business.
