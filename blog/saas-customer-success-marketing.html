What is Customer Success Marketing?

Customer success marketing is every marketing activity that happens after someone becomes a customer. Its goals:

  • Reduce churn: Keep customers using and paying
  • Increase adoption: Drive usage of features
  • Drive expansion: Upsells, cross-sells, seat expansion
  • Create advocates: Turn customers into referrers and promoters
Why it matters now: In 2026, CAC is higher than ever. The SaaS companies winning are those with 120%+ NRR (net revenue retention). That comes from customer success marketing, not just acquisition.

The Customer Lifecycle Marketing Framework

1 Onboarding (Days 1-30)

The most critical phase. 70% of churn happens because customers never got value from the product.

  • Welcome sequence: 5-7 emails guiding to activation
  • Product education: Video tutorials, help docs, webinars
  • Milestone celebrations: Celebrate first achievement
  • Check-in campaigns: "How's it going?" at day 7, 14, 30
  • Goal: Get to activation metric ASAP
2 Adoption (Days 30-90)

Customer is using the product, but not deeply. Your job: make them power users.

  • Feature education: "Did you know you can..."
  • Use case content: Show new ways to use the product
  • Integration prompts: Connect other tools they use
  • Power user webinars: Advanced tips and tricks
  • Goal: Increase product stickiness
3 Retention (Ongoing)

Active customers need continued engagement to prevent churn.

  • Product updates: New features, improvements
  • ROI reports: Show the value they're getting
  • Health score monitoring: Trigger campaigns for at-risk accounts
  • Community building: User groups, Slack communities
  • Goal: Maintain engagement, prevent silent churn
4 Expansion (Months 3+)

Happy customers are ready for more. This is your expansion revenue engine.

  • Upgrade campaigns: "You're hitting limits on X plan"
  • Add-on promotions: Cross-sell complementary products
  • Seat expansion: "Add your team" campaigns
  • Annual conversion: Monthly → annual discounts
  • Goal: Increase revenue per customer
5 Advocacy (After Success)

Your best customers should become your best marketers.

  • Review requests: G2, Capterra, TrustRadius
  • Referral programs: Incentivize introductions
  • Case studies: Document their success
  • Speaking opportunities: Webinars, events, podcasts
  • Goal: Turn customers into acquisition channels

Essential Customer Success Campaigns

1. The Onboarding Email Sequence

A 7-email sequence over 14 days:

  1. Day 0: Welcome + immediate next step
  2. Day 1: Quick win tutorial (video)
  3. Day 3: Feature spotlight #1
  4. Day 5: Case study / social proof
  5. Day 7: Check-in: "How's it going?"
  6. Day 10: Feature spotlight #2
  7. Day 14: Success resources roundup

2. The At-Risk Customer Campaign

Triggered when health score drops or usage declines:

  • Email 1: "We noticed you haven't logged in..."
  • Email 2: Offer help—free training session
  • Email 3: Share quick wins others are getting
  • CS call: Personal outreach from CSM
  • Executive sponsor: If high-value account, escalate

3. The Expansion Campaign

Triggered based on usage patterns:

  • Usage-based: "You've used 80% of your API calls"
  • Feature-based: "Unlock [advanced feature] with Pro"
  • Team-based: "3 colleagues viewed your report—add them?"
  • Time-based: Anniversary offers, renewal upgrades

4. The NPS/Review Campaign

Turn satisfaction into visible social proof:

  1. Send NPS survey: After activation milestone
  2. For promoters (9-10): Ask for G2/Capterra review
  3. For passives (7-8): Ask what would make it a 10
  4. For detractors (0-6): Trigger CS outreach immediately

Churn Prevention Strategies

Identify At-Risk Customers Early

Build a health score based on:

  • Product usage: Login frequency, feature adoption
  • Support tickets: Volume and sentiment
  • Billing issues: Failed payments, downgrades
  • Engagement: Email opens, webinar attendance
  • Relationship: Executive sponsor changes, contact turnover

Proactive Intervention Playbook

Risk Signal Action
No login in 14 days Automated re-engagement email
No login in 30 days CSM personal outreach call
3+ support tickets (negative) Priority support + CSM check-in
Champion leaves company Immediate new stakeholder mapping
Downgrade request Save call + offer retention incentive

Building a Customer Marketing Program

Content for Customers

  • Customer newsletter: Monthly product updates, tips, success stories
  • Help center: Searchable docs, video tutorials
  • Academy: Structured learning paths and certifications
  • Community: Forums, Slack, user groups
  • Customer events: User conferences, local meetups

Advocacy Program Structure

  1. Identify advocates: High NPS, active users, vocal fans
  2. Create tiers: Bronze (review), Silver (case study), Gold (speaker)
  3. Offer rewards: Swag, discounts, exclusive access, public recognition
  4. Make it easy: Pre-written reviews, speaking briefs, referral links
  5. Track and thank: Measure impact, show gratitude

Metrics That Matter

  • Net Revenue Retention (NRR): Target 120%+ (top quartile)
  • Gross Revenue Retention: Target 90%+ (before expansion)
  • Logo churn: % of customers lost (target <5% annually)
  • Expansion revenue: % of revenue from upsells/cross-sells
  • Time to value: Days from signup to activation
  • NPS: Target 40+ (excellent for B2B SaaS)
  • Review velocity: New G2/Capterra reviews per month

Quick Wins: Start This Week

  1. Audit your onboarding emails—are they focused on activation?
  2. Set up a "no login in 14 days" trigger email
  3. Ask your 5 happiest customers for G2 reviews
  4. Create a simple health score in your CRM
  5. Send a "what else can we help with?" email to active users
  6. Launch a basic referral program (even just asking for intros)
