Retargeting & Remarketing: How to Convert Visitors Who Left Your Site

By Great Hornbills Team • March 2026 • 6 min read

What Is Retargeting?

Retargeting (also called remarketing) is the practice of showing ads to people who have previously visited your website or interacted with your brand but did not convert. Only 2-3% of first-time visitors convert, meaning 97% leave without taking action. Retargeting brings them back. Combined with optimized landing pages, retargeting can increase conversion rates by 70%.

Setting Up Retargeting Pixels

Install tracking pixels from Google Ads (Google Tag), Meta (Facebook Pixel), and any other advertising platforms you use. Place these in your website's header before launching any campaigns. Use Google Tag Manager for easy management of multiple pixels. Verify pixel firing using browser extensions like Meta Pixel Helper.

Retargeting Audience Segments

Create segmented audiences based on behavior: homepage visitors (broadest), product page viewers, cart abandoners (highest intent), past purchasers (for upselling), and blog readers. Each segment receives different messaging. Cart abandoners might see a discount offer, while product viewers see benefit-focused ads.

Retargeting Ad Strategies That Work

Show dynamic product ads to visitors who viewed specific products. Use sequential messaging that tells a story across multiple ad impressions. Combine retargeting with email remarketing for multi-channel touchpoints. Offer increasing incentives to non-converters over time.

Frequency Capping and Ad Fatigue Prevention

Set frequency caps of 3-5 impressions per user per week to prevent ad fatigue. Rotate creative every 2-3 weeks. Exclude users who have already converted. Set membership durations based on your sales cycle — 7-14 days for impulse purchases, 30-90 days for considered purchases.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between retargeting and remarketing?

The terms are often used interchangeably. Technically, retargeting refers to serving ads to previous website visitors through display and social advertising platforms. Remarketing traditionally refers to re-engaging customers through email. In practice, both terms describe the strategy of re-engaging people who have previously interacted with your brand.

How effective is retargeting?

Retargeting is one of the most cost-effective advertising strategies. Retargeted visitors are 70% more likely to convert than first-time visitors. Retargeting ads have 10x higher click-through rates than standard display ads. The average ROI for retargeting campaigns is significantly higher than prospecting campaigns.

How soon should I retarget website visitors?

Begin retargeting within 24 hours of the visit for highest effectiveness. Cart abandonment retargeting should trigger within 1-4 hours. Set different retargeting windows based on your sales cycle and audience segment. The retargeting window should match how long your purchase decision typically takes.
