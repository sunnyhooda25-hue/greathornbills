SaaS Pricing Strategies: How to Price Your Product for Growth

March 11, 2026 • 12 min read

Pricing is the most powerful lever in your business. A 1% improvement in pricing leads to an 11% increase in profits—more than any other lever. Yet most SaaS founders spend less time on pricing than on their homepage design.

The Three Pricing Models

1. Per-Seat Pricing

Charge based on the number of users.

Best for: Collaboration tools, productivity software, CRMs

Examples: Slack, Asana, Salesforce

2. Usage-Based Pricing

Charge based on consumption (API calls, events, storage).

Best for: Infrastructure, APIs, platforms with variable usage

Examples: AWS, Twilio, Stripe

3. Feature-Based Pricing

Charge based on which features are included.

Best for: Products with clear feature differentiation by segment

Examples: HubSpot, Notion, Figma

Value-Based Pricing Framework

The most powerful approach is to price based on value delivered, not cost incurred. Here's how:

  1. Quantify the value: What's the ROI of using your product?
  2. Capture a fraction: Price at 10-20% of value delivered
  3. Segment by value: Different customers get different value—price accordingly

The 3-Tier Structure

Most SaaS companies use 3 pricing tiers. Here's why it works:

  • Entry tier: Low barrier to entry, acquires customers
  • Middle tier: Where most customers land (the "decoy effect")
  • Enterprise tier: For larger customers, often custom pricing

Common Pricing Mistakes

  1. Pricing too low: Most SaaS underprices by 50% or more
  2. Too many tiers: More than 4 tiers creates confusion
  3. Feature gating frustration: Don't gate features that feel essential
  4. Ignoring willingness to pay: Talk to customers about pricing
  5. Never changing prices: You should revisit pricing annually

When to Raise Prices

Signs you should raise prices:

  • Close rate is above 30% (you're too cheap)
  • No price objections in sales calls
  • Customers say you're "great value"
  • Competitors charge significantly more
  • You've added major features without adjusting price

Pricing for PLG vs. Sales-Led

Product-Led Growth: Price needs to be transparent, low-friction to start, with clear upgrade paths.

Sales-Led: Can use custom pricing, require demos, and negotiate based on value.
