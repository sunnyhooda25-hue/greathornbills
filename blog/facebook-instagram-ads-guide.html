Facebook & Instagram Ads: The Complete Guide for Brands in 2026

By Great Hornbills Team • March 2026 • 6 min read

Why Advertise on Facebook and Instagram?

Meta's advertising platform reaches over 3.7 billion monthly active users across Facebook and Instagram. The platform offers the most sophisticated audience targeting available, including detailed demographics, interests, behaviors, and retargeting capabilities. Average ROAS for well-optimized campaigns is 3-5x.

Campaign Structure and Objectives

Meta ads use a three-tier structure: Campaign (objective), Ad Set (targeting and budget), and Ad (creative). Choose your objective based on your goal: Awareness for brand reach, Traffic for website visits, Engagement for social interaction, Leads for form fills, or Sales for purchases.

Audience Targeting Strategies

Build your targeting strategy in layers. Start with core audiences based on demographics and interests. Create custom audiences from your customer list, website visitors, and app users. Then build lookalike audiences that mirror your best customers. Layer exclusions to avoid showing ads to existing customers when prospecting.

Ad Formats and Creative Best Practices

Use UGC-style video ads for highest performance — user-generated content ads consistently outperform polished brand creative. Carousel ads work well for showcasing multiple products. Stories and Reels placements are increasingly important. Test at least 3-5 creative variations per ad set.

Budget, Bidding, and Optimization

Start with $20-50 per day per ad set during testing. Use Advantage+ campaigns for broad targeting with Meta's AI optimization. Monitor frequency to avoid ad fatigue (keep below 3 for cold audiences). Scale winning ad sets by 20% every 3-4 days to avoid resetting the learning phase.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much should I spend on Facebook ads?

Start with a minimum test budget of $1,000-$2,000 per month to gather enough data for optimization. Most successful brands spend $3,000-$10,000 per month on Meta advertising. Your ideal budget depends on your cost per acquisition targets and total addressable audience size.

What is a good ROAS for Facebook ads?

A good ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) depends on your margins. Generally, 3x ROAS is considered good, 4-5x is very good, and 6x+ is excellent. For ecommerce, account for your cost of goods — you need a minimum ROAS of 2-3x to be profitable after product and fulfillment costs.

Are Facebook ads still worth it in 2026?

Yes. Despite privacy changes, Meta's advertising platform remains the most cost-effective for reaching consumers at scale. Advantage+ AI campaigns have improved targeting effectiveness. The key is strong creative (especially video and UGC), proper conversion tracking, and patient optimization.
