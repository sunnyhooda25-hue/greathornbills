Online Reputation Management: How to Protect and Build Your Brand in 2026

By Great Hornbills Team • March 2026 • 6 min read

What Is Online Reputation Management?

Online Reputation Management (ORM) is the practice of monitoring, influencing, and managing how your brand is perceived across the internet. This includes search engine results, social media mentions, review sites, news articles, and forum discussions. In 2026, 93% of consumers read online reviews before making a purchase, and 94% say a negative review has convinced them to avoid a business.

Monitoring Your Brand Online

Set up Google Alerts for your brand name, key personnel, and product names. Use social listening tools like Brandwatch or Mention to track mentions across social media, news sites, blogs, and forums. Monitor review platforms relevant to your industry including Google Business Profile, Yelp, and Trustpilot.

Responding to Negative Reviews

How you respond to negative reviews is more important than avoiding them. Respond within 24 hours. Acknowledge the customer's experience without being defensive. Apologize for genuine shortcomings. Offer to resolve the issue through direct communication. Potential customers read your responses — a professional response can turn a negative into a positive.

Building a Positive Online Presence

Proactively build positive reputation through consistent content publishing, actively requesting reviews from satisfied customers, engaging in thought leadership, maintaining active social media profiles, and publishing case studies and success stories.

Crisis Communication Planning

Every brand should have a crisis communication plan before they need one. Identify potential reputation risks. Designate a crisis response team. Prepare template responses for common scenarios. Establish an escalation process. Practice your response plan annually.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to repair a damaged online reputation?

Repairing an online reputation typically takes 3-12 months depending on the severity and scale of the damage. Minor negative review issues can be addressed in weeks through response management and positive review generation. Major reputation crises involving news coverage may take 6-12 months of sustained positive content creation and PR efforts.

Can negative reviews be removed from Google?

Google only removes reviews that violate their policies (spam, fake, offensive, or conflicts of interest). You can flag policy-violating reviews for removal. For legitimate negative reviews, the best approach is to respond professionally and generate more positive reviews to improve your overall rating.

How important are online reviews for business?

Extremely important. 93% of consumers read online reviews before purchasing. Businesses with 4+ star ratings earn 12% more revenue than those below 4 stars. Even a 0.1-star improvement in average rating can increase conversion rates by 25%. Online reviews are one of the most influential factors in consumer decision-making.
