Google Ads vs Facebook Ads: Which Is Better for Your Business?

By Great Hornbills Team • March 2026 • 6 min read

The Fundamental Difference

Google Ads captures existing demand — people actively searching for your product or service. Facebook/Instagram Ads create demand — reaching people based on interests before they search. Both are essential components of a comprehensive digital marketing strategy.

When to Use Google Ads

Choose Google Ads when your product solves a known problem people actively search for, when purchase intent is high, when you want to capture demand at the moment of decision, for local service businesses, and when targeting B2B buyers researching solutions. Google Ads typically delivers faster conversions with higher intent.

When to Use Facebook Ads

Choose Facebook/Instagram Ads when you need to build brand awareness, when selling visually appealing or impulse-purchase products, when targeting specific demographic or interest-based audiences, for retargeting website visitors, and when launching new products that people don't yet know to search for.

Cost Comparison in 2026

Google Search Ads average CPC: $2-5 (varies widely by industry). Facebook/Instagram average CPC: $0.50-2.00. Google typically has higher CPC but higher conversion rates. Facebook offers lower CPC with broader reach. The best approach for most businesses is using both platforms strategically. Plan your budget using our budget planning guide.

The Best Strategy: Use Both

Successful brands use Google Ads to capture high-intent search traffic and Facebook/Instagram Ads for awareness, prospecting, and retargeting. Allocate 50-60% to your highest-performing channel and 40-50% to the supporting channel. Test and adjust based on your specific performance data.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I use Google Ads or Facebook Ads first?

If people actively search for what you sell, start with Google Ads to capture existing demand. If you are launching something new or need to build awareness, start with Facebook Ads. Most businesses benefit from launching both simultaneously with a split test budget.

Which platform is cheaper — Google or Facebook?

Facebook typically has lower cost per click ($0.50-2.00 vs $2-5 on Google). However, Google often delivers higher conversion rates due to higher search intent. Cost-effectiveness depends on your specific industry, conversion rates, and customer lifetime value. Calculate cost per acquisition on both.

Can Google Ads and Facebook Ads work together?

Absolutely. The most effective strategy uses Google Ads to capture high-intent searches and Facebook Ads for prospecting and retargeting. Run Facebook awareness campaigns to introduce your brand, then capture interested users with Google Search ads when they research further.
