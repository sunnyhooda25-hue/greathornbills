Social Media Analytics: 15 Metrics Every Brand Must Track in 2026

By Great Hornbills Team • March 2026 • 6 min read

Why Social Media Analytics Matter

Without analytics, your social media strategy is guesswork. Data-driven brands outperform competitors by 23% in customer acquisition and 19% in profitability. Understanding which metrics to track and how to interpret them transforms social media from a cost center into a measurable revenue driver.

Engagement Metrics (1-5)

1. Engagement Rate: Total engagements divided by impressions — the single most important metric for content quality. Aim for 3-6% on Instagram, 5-9% on TikTok, and 2-4% on LinkedIn. 2. Comments: The highest-value engagement signal as algorithms heavily weight conversation. 3. Shares/Reposts: Indicates content resonated enough for users to endorse it to their network. 4. Saves: Particularly valuable on Instagram — high save rates signal content has lasting reference value. 5. Video Watch Time: Average percentage viewed and replay rate indicate content quality.

Reach and Awareness Metrics (6-10)

6. Impressions: Total number of times your content was displayed. 7. Reach: Unique accounts that saw your content. 8. Follower Growth Rate: Monthly percentage increase in followers — more meaningful than absolute count. 9. Share of Voice: How your brand mentions compare to competitors in your industry. 10. Brand Mention Sentiment: Whether mentions are positive, negative, or neutral.

Conversion Metrics (11-15)

11. Click-Through Rate (CTR): Percentage of viewers who clicked your link — benchmark is 1-3%. 12. Conversion Rate: Percentage of social visitors who complete a desired action. 13. Cost Per Lead (CPL): Total ad spend divided by leads generated. 14. Return on Ad Spend (ROAS): Revenue generated per dollar of ad spend — aim for 3x minimum. Learn about optimizing your social ad campaigns. 15. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC): Total marketing cost to acquire one new customer.

Tools for Social Media Analytics

Use native platform analytics (Instagram Insights, TikTok Analytics, LinkedIn Analytics) for basic metrics. For advanced cross-platform reporting, tools like Sprout Social, Hootsuite Analytics, and Google Analytics 4 provide comprehensive dashboards. Set up UTM parameters on all social links to track traffic and conversions accurately in Google Analytics.

Using Data to Improve Strategy

Review metrics weekly and conduct monthly performance reviews. Identify your top-performing content types, posting times, and topics. Double down on what works and reduce what doesn't. Use A/B testing on ad creative, copy, and audiences to continuously optimize performance.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most important social media metric to track?

Engagement rate is the single most important metric because it indicates content quality and audience resonance. However, for business results, track conversion metrics like click-through rate and cost per lead alongside engagement to connect social media activity to revenue.

How often should I check social media analytics?

Monitor high-level metrics (engagement rate, reach) weekly. Conduct detailed performance analysis monthly. Review campaign-specific metrics in real-time during active campaigns. Quarterly reviews should assess overall strategy effectiveness and inform planning for the next quarter.

What is a good engagement rate on social media?

Good engagement rates vary by platform: Instagram 3-6%, TikTok 5-9%, LinkedIn 2-4%, Facebook 1-3%, and X 0.5-1%. Rates above these ranges indicate strong content performance. Micro-influencers and smaller accounts typically see higher engagement rates than large accounts.
