Generative Engine Optimization (GEO): How to Get Your Brand Cited by AI

By Great Hornbills Team • March 2026 • 6 min read

What Is Generative Engine Optimization?

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the practice of optimizing your digital content so that AI-powered search platforms — including ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, Claude, and Microsoft Copilot — can discover, reference, and recommend your brand. As AI assistants handle an estimated 25% of all search queries by late 2026, GEO has become as important as traditional SEO.

How AI Search Differs from Traditional Search

Traditional search engines rank web pages as a list of links. AI search engines synthesize information from multiple sources into a single conversational answer, typically citing 2-7 sources per response. Success in AI search is measured by mention rate — how frequently your brand appears across AI-generated responses.

Technical GEO Requirements

Ensure AI crawlers can access your content by checking that your robots.txt does not block GPTBot, ClaudeBot, and Google-Extended. Use server-side rendering. Implement schema markup — particularly Article, Organization, FAQ, HowTo, and Breadcrumb schema. Consider creating an llms.txt file for machine-readable content summaries.

Content Optimization for AI Citation

AI models prioritize content with unique value and information gain. Include original statistics, proprietary research, and novel frameworks. Structure content with clear definitions that retain meaning as standalone paragraphs. Write in a factual, authoritative tone with specific numbers. Use the same content marketing principles but structure for extraction.

Building Entity Authority for AI

Build your brand's entity presence by maintaining consistent information across the web, claiming your Google Knowledge Panel, earning mentions from authoritative publications, publishing thought leadership under named experts, and building presence on platforms AI models frequently reference.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is GEO in marketing?

GEO stands for Generative Engine Optimization. It is the practice of optimizing your brand's digital content so that AI-powered search engines and assistants — like ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Claude, and Perplexity — can discover, understand, and cite your brand when generating answers to user queries.

How is GEO different from SEO?

Traditional SEO optimizes for ranking in search engine results pages (link lists). GEO optimizes for being cited in AI-generated answers. While both require quality content and technical optimization, GEO specifically focuses on structured data, entity authority, information gain, and content that AI can extract as standalone factual statements.

How do I know if AI is citing my brand?

Search for your brand name and relevant queries on ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Claude. Note how often your brand appears in responses. Tools like Profound and Otterly.ai are emerging to track AI citation rates. Monitor whether AI assistants recommend your brand when users ask about your product category.
