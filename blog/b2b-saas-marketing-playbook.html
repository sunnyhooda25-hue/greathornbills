The B2B SaaS Marketing Playbook: From $0 to $1M ARR

March 11, 2026 • 15 min read

Marketing a B2B SaaS company is fundamentally different at each stage of growth. The tactics that work at $0 ARR will break at $100K ARR. Here's the complete playbook for each stage.

Stage 1: Pre-Launch ($0 ARR)

1 Build Audience Before Product

Your goal isn't marketing—it's learning. But you can build an audience while you learn.

  • Start a waitlist: Use a simple landing page to capture interest
  • Build in public: Share your journey on Twitter/LinkedIn
  • Talk to potential customers: 50+ conversations minimum
  • Join communities: Be helpful in Slack groups, Reddit, etc.

Budget: $0-500/month (just hosting and basic tools)

Stage 2: Finding PMF ($0-$10K MRR)

2 Founder-Led Everything

At this stage, founders should be doing most sales and marketing. You're still learning what resonates.

  • Manual outbound: Founders sending personalized emails/DMs
  • Content from the trenches: Write about what you're learning
  • Early adopter communities: Product Hunt, Hacker News, niche forums
  • Referrals: Ask every customer for intros

Budget: $500-2K/month

Stage 3: Early Traction ($10K-$50K MRR)

3 Find Your Channel

You've proven people will pay. Now find one channel that works and go deep.

  • Double down on what's working: If LinkedIn is driving leads, do more LinkedIn
  • Start content engine: 2-4 blog posts per month, SEO-focused
  • Test paid acquisition: Small experiments on Google/LinkedIn/Facebook
  • Build case studies: Document every customer win

Budget: $3K-10K/month

Stage 4: Scaling ($50K-$200K MRR)

4 Build the Machine

Time to systematize. What the founder did manually now needs to become a repeatable process.

  • Hire first marketer: Generalist who can execute across channels
  • Scale winning channel: 2-3x investment in what's working
  • Add second channel: Start experimenting with channel #2
  • Marketing automation: Nurture sequences, lead scoring, CRM

Budget: $15K-40K/month

Stage 5: Growth ($200K-$1M MRR)

5 Multi-Channel Expansion

You have product-market fit and a working acquisition engine. Time to pour fuel on the fire.

  • Build marketing team: Specialists for content, paid, product marketing
  • Scale paid acquisition: Significant budget for proven channels
  • Brand building: Start investing in awareness, not just performance
  • International expansion: Localize for new markets

Budget: $50K-200K/month

Key Metrics by Stage

Focus on these metrics at each stage:

  • Pre-launch: Waitlist signups, conversations had
  • $0-10K MRR: Trial signups, activation rate, time to value
  • $10-50K MRR: CAC, payback period, MQL volume
  • $50-200K MRR: CAC by channel, LTV:CAC, pipeline velocity
  • $200K+ MRR: Blended CAC, brand awareness, NPS

Common Mistakes

  1. Scaling too early: Don't pour money into ads before you have PMF
  2. Too many channels: Better to master one channel than be mediocre at five
  3. Ignoring retention: Marketing doesn't stop at acquisition
  4. No attribution: If you can't measure it, you can't optimize it
  5. Copying others: What works for Stripe won't necessarily work for you
