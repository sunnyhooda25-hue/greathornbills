Frequently Asked Questions

What is SaaS marketing?
SaaS marketing is the process of promoting and selling software-as-a-service products through digital channels. It focuses on acquiring, converting, and retaining customers through strategies like content marketing, paid advertising, SEO, and product-led growth, with a focus on recurring revenue metrics like MRR, CAC, and LTV.
How much does SaaS marketing cost?
SaaS marketing costs vary based on scope and goals. Most SaaS companies invest 20-50% of revenue in sales and marketing. Agency retainers typically range from $2,000-$15,000/month depending on services included. The key is ensuring your CAC payback period stays under 12 months.
What metrics matter most for SaaS marketing?
The most important SaaS marketing metrics are: Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC), Lifetime Value (LTV), LTV:CAC ratio (aim for 3:1+), CAC payback period (under 12 months), Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR), churn rate, and trial-to-paid conversion rate.
How long does it take to see results?
Paid channels like Google Ads and LinkedIn can show results in 2-4 weeks. SEO typically takes 3-6 months to show meaningful rankings. Content marketing compounds over 6-12 months. We recommend a mix of short-term paid and long-term organic strategies.
What's the difference between PLG and sales-led SaaS marketing?
Product-Led Growth (PLG) focuses on the product as the primary driver of acquisition, with free trials and freemium models. Sales-led focuses on outbound sales with marketing supporting lead generation. Many successful SaaS companies use a hybrid approach combining both strategies.
